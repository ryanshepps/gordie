# Marketing Strategy

## Value Prop

Gordie helps fantasy players come up with more complex strategies and spend less time executing on them (comparing players, checking availability, etc.).

### Key Insight: Casual Players Don't Want Optimization

Casual players play for fun with friends. They aren't looking for a tool — they want to win with their own brain. They're not competitive enough to seek help. Selling them "AI-powered lineup optimization" won't convert.

### Reframe: Entertainment First, Tool Second

The way to convert casual players isn't to convince them they need help. It's to **make them less casual** by increasing their engagement with fantasy.

Think sports talk radio — nobody listens to Stephen A. Smith because they need help understanding basketball. They listen because it's entertaining, gives them takes to argue about, and makes them feel more connected to the sport.

## Freemium Model: Digest (Free) + Interaction (Paid)

### Free Tier: Game-Day Digest

A free digest that makes fantasy more fun to follow. Not a tool — content.

- Hot takes, surprising stats, trash talk fodder
- Opinionated and personality-driven (Gordie has a voice)
- Generic league-wide advice (not roster-specific)
- Goal: make readers think about fantasy more, care more, engage more

The digest must be **worth sharing** — the bar is "would someone screenshot this and send it to their group chat?"

### Paid Tier: Interactive AI Assistant

Once a casual player starts caring more about winning (because the digest made them more engaged), they upgrade to interact with Gordie directly.

- Text Gordie with questions anytime
- Roster-specific analysis (knows your league, your players, your waiver wire)
- Personalized start/sit, trade evaluation, waiver recommendations
- "Gordie manages your team" vs. "Gordie tells you what's happening"

### The Conversion Path

1. Gordie's digest is fun to read — hot takes, trash talk fodder, surprising stats
2. Reading it makes the user more engaged with their league
3. They start checking their lineup more, talking trash in the group chat
4. They actually start caring about winning
5. They want Gordie to look at *their* roster — upgrade to paid

The free tier creates the demand for the paid tier organically.

### Free vs. Paid Comparison

| Free | Paid |
|------|------|
| Game-day digest with Gordie's take | Text Gordie anytime with questions |
| Generic league-wide advice | Roster-specific analysis |
| "Here's what's happening" | "Here's what *you* should do" |
| "Pacheco is out — his backup is a must-add" | "Pacheco is out. He's on your bench. Hunt is available in your league — drop Warren for him" |
| Read-only | Interactive |

## Conversion Strategies for Casual Players

### Casual Player Segments

Casual players aren't one group. Each type has a different conversion trigger:

| Type | Motivation | Conversion Trigger |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Social player** | Plays because friends do | Their friends use Gordie ("I'm the only one without it") |
| **Set-and-forget player** | Sets lineup once, checks score later | A preventable loss — started an injured player |
| **Curious tinkerer** | Enjoys the strategy but doesn't obsess | Discovers Gordie does something they didn't know was possible |

### Strategy: League Trojan Horse (Network Effects + Mimetic Desire)

Fantasy leagues are 8-12 person social graphs. You don't need to convert the casual player directly — you need to convert **one person in their league**.

**How it works:**
1. One person in a league starts using Gordie and starts winning
2. Others notice ("how did you know to pick up that guy?")
3. "Oh, I use this AI thing called Gordie"
4. Now it's not "do I need AI?" — it's "my opponent has an unfair advantage"

**Referral program design:**
- Don't reward per-signup. Reward per-league penetration.
- "Get 3 people in your league on Gordie, everyone gets a free month."
- Leverages **competitive anxiety** and **social proof** simultaneously
- The casual player doesn't upgrade because they want to win more — they upgrade because they don't want to be the only one *without* it

This is mimetic desire — people want things because others want them. The casual player never has to admit they "need help." They just don't want to be at a disadvantage.

### Strategy: Zero-Friction Shareable Answers (Endowment Effect + IKEA Effect)

Instead of requiring someone to sign up for a digest, make the first interaction require literally nothing.

**How it works:**
1. Someone shares a Gordie link in a group chat: `gordie.ai/start-sit/williams-vs-achane`
2. It's a free, instant answer. No signup. No email.
3. At the bottom: "Ask Gordie your own question"
4. They type a question. Get one answer. Free.
5. NOW: "Want Gordie to watch your roster every week? Enter your email."

**Why this works:**
- **Endowment effect**: Once someone has used Gordie even once, they value it more
- **IKEA effect**: Typing their own question and getting a personalized answer creates more investment than passively reading a digest
- **Shareable unit**: A single start/sit answer is easy to drop in a group chat. A digest is harder to share.

The shareable answer page gives you a viral unit. The league dynamics give you a natural expansion mechanism. Neither requires the casual player to admit they need help.

### Strongest Combined Play

The zero-friction shareable answer (acquisition) + league referral dynamics (expansion) is the strongest combination. The digest works better as step 2 (retention) rather than step 1 (acquisition):

1. **Acquire**: Shareable answer pages spread through group chats
2. **Retain**: Free digest keeps them engaged week over week
3. **Expand**: League referral program turns one user into many
4. **Convert**: Loss aversion moments + competitive anxiety trigger paid upgrades

## Marketing Channels (Pre-Launch, Solo, Free/Low Budget)

### 1. Reddit Marketing

r/fantasyfootball has 2M+ members. Free, and it's where the audience lives.

- Lurk and identify recurring pain points
- Answer questions using Gordie behind the scenes — share the answers, not the tool
- Build credibility, then share Gordie naturally when relevant
- Learn the language the audience uses to describe their problems

### 2. Waitlist with Referral Loop

Create urgency and build an email list before launch.

- Landing page with the sharpened value prop
- "Join the waitlist" with email capture
- "Share to move up" with a referral link
- Tools: Waitliful, or build into the SvelteKit frontend

### 3. Short-Form Video (TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts)

Fantasy + AI is a natural curiosity hook.

- Screen-record asking Gordie real questions ("Who do I start, Williams or Achane?")
- Show the answer. Keep it under 60 seconds.
- Post same video across TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
- Even 5-10k views can drive meaningful waitlist signups

### 4. Fantasy Podcast Tour

Hundreds of small-to-mid fantasy podcasts need guests. "I built an AI fantasy assistant" is a compelling story.

- Target podcasts with under 10k listeners (they'll respond)
- Pitch: "I built an AI that plays fantasy football — here's what it taught me"
- Time for July-August (pre-draft season)
- Each appearance = 50-200 signups with a waitlist ready

### 5. Engineering as Marketing — Free Tool

Build one free, ungated tool that solves a specific fantasy problem.

- Ideas: Trade Value Calculator, Start/Sit tool, Draft Grade Analyzer
- Free, no login required
- Subtle "Powered by Gordie" CTA
- SEO traffic + social sharing during fantasy season

## Priority Order

1. Fix the value prop — test "entertainment-first" positioning with real people
2. Build the waitlist page — need somewhere to send people
3. Start posting on Reddit — learn audience language and pain points
4. Record 3-5 short-form videos — bank content for when ready to drive traffic
5. Queue up podcast outreach for July — draft season is peak attention
