# Launch Strategy

## Timeline

- **Now → June**: Expand sports, build channels, refine product with testers
- **July → August**: Alpha/beta launch, build waitlist, create content
- **September**: Full launch timed to fantasy draft season
- **October+**: Ongoing launch moments tied to weekly fantasy cycles

---

## App Changes Ranked by Conversion Impact

### Tier 1: High Impact (Do These First)

#### 1. Add a referral loop

Fantasy is inherently viral. Every league has 8-16 managers. If one person uses Gordie and wins, others want in.

- After a user connects their Yahoo league, show them: "Invite your leaguemates — get 1 free week of Standard for each signup"
- Include a referral link in every weekly digest email: "Share Gordie with your league"
- Incentive: extended trial days or a discount on Standard

This is the single highest-leverage change because fantasy players already talk to each other weekly.

#### 2. Rework the trial-to-paid transition

Currently: trial expires → silently drops to Free tier (3 questions/week). This is a soft wall most people will just live with.

- **Day 7 email**: "Here's what Gordie has done for your team this week" — summarize value delivered (questions answered, lineup changes suggested, waiver picks)
- **Day 11 email**: "Your trial ends in 3 days" — show what they'll lose (digests, unlimited questions, multi-league)
- **Day 14**: Send a "Your trial ended" email with a **time-limited offer** — "Upgrade in the next 48 hours for 30% off your first month"
- **Free tier interaction**: When a free user hits their 3-question limit, respond with a nudge: "You've used your 3 free questions this week. Upgrade to keep the conversation going → [link]"

Goal: make the value gap between Free and Standard feel painful, not invisible.

#### 3. Add social proof to the landing page

- Testimonials (even informal — "Gordie told me to pick up Bedard off waivers before anyone else in my league noticed")
- A "live" stat counter: "X questions answered" or "X lineups optimized"
- Screenshot of an actual Gordie email response showing the quality of advice

Fantasy players are skeptical of AI tools. Seeing a real, high-quality response is more convincing than any feature description.

#### 4. Rewrite the CTA and hero copy for specificity

"Your AI fantasy sports assistant" is generic. Fantasy players respond to specific, confident claims:

- "Gordie told 73% of users to start the right goalie last week" (once data exists)
- "Your fantasy hockey scout that never sleeps"
- "Get waiver wire picks before your leaguemates wake up"

Add a subline under the CTA: **"14-day free trial. No credit card. Cancel anytime."**

### Tier 2: Medium Impact (Build Before Launch)

#### 5. Create a "coming soon" waitlist for new sports

- Add a section: "Gordie is expanding. Which sport do you want next?"
- Let people vote (football/basketball/baseball/soccer) and enter their email
- Builds email list, segments by sport interest, creates anticipation
- When each sport launches, there's a warm list to announce to

#### 6. Build one owned channel: a weekly email newsletter

"Gordie's Weekly Wire" — top waiver pickups, start/sit calls, trending players:

- Publish as a blog post AND send as an email
- Triple duty: SEO content, email list growth, demonstrates Gordie's intelligence to non-users
- CTA in every issue: "Want personalized advice for YOUR team? Try Gordie free"

Best single channel to build because it compounds — every issue adds SEO value and every subscriber is a potential conversion.

#### 7. Add an interactive demo or sample conversation

The landing page describes features but doesn't show them:

- A mock email thread showing a user asking "Should I start Demko or Swayman tonight?" and Gordie's actual response
- Or let visitors type a sample question and see a pre-built response (curated examples, not live AI)

### Tier 3: Launch Amplifiers (September)

#### 8. Product Hunt + Reddit launch

Time Product Hunt for late September (fantasy draft week):

- r/fantasyhockey — become a regular contributor over the summer, then share Gordie when the season starts
- Product Hunt for broader tech audience
- Hacker News "Show HN" for the technical angle (AI agent that works via email)

#### 9. Seasonal urgency mechanics

Add time-based urgency during draft season:

- "Draft season is here — connect your league before Week 1"
- "Your league's draft is in X days" (if detectable from Yahoo)
- Countdown to season start

#### 10. League-level onboarding

Instead of one user at a time, let someone invite their whole league:

- "Share this link with your league — everyone gets a 14-day trial"
- Turns one signup into 8-16 potential users

## What NOT to Change

- Email-first approach is a genuine differentiator. Don't add a dashboard — lean into simplicity.
- 14-day no-credit-card trial is the right call for this stage. Don't gate it harder.
- Pricing tiers are reasonable. $10/mo for fantasy advice is an easy impulse buy.
